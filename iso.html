About ISO

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) is an independent, non-governmental international body that develops and publishes global standards to ensure the quality, safety, and efficiency of products, services, and systems.

ISO standards are widely used in the pharmaceutical, healthcare, and manufacturing industries to maintain regulatory compliance and promote global harmonization of quality management systems.

Main Responsibilities

  • Develop and publish international standards for quality, safety, and efficiency.
  • Provide frameworks for quality management (e.g., ISO 9001) and medical device safety (ISO 13485).
  • Promote harmonization of international regulations and best practices.
  • Collaborate with organizations such as WHO, ICH, and regulatory bodies worldwide.
  • Support sustainable and innovation-driven development through standardization.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of ISO standards?

ISO standards provide globally recognized specifications that ensure products and services are safe, reliable, and of good quality.

Which ISO standards are relevant to pharmaceuticals?

Key standards include ISO 9001 (Quality Management Systems), ISO 13485 (Medical Devices), and ISO 14644 (Cleanrooms and associated environments).

Is ISO a regulatory body?

No, ISO is not a regulatory agency but a voluntary standard-setting body. However, many regulatory authorities reference ISO standards.

How can companies get ISO certified?

Organizations can obtain certification through accredited bodies that assess compliance with specific ISO standards such as ISO 9001 or 13485.
