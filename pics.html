About PIC/S

The Pharmaceutical Inspection Co-operation Scheme (PIC/S) is an international organization that promotes global harmonization in Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) standards and inspection procedures for pharmaceuticals. It brings together regulatory authorities from around the world to improve cooperation and mutual confidence in GMP compliance.

PIC/S facilitates collaboration between regulatory agencies, supports the development of training programs for inspectors, and works to ensure consistent implementation of GMP worldwide.

Main Responsibilities

  • Promote harmonized GMP standards and inspection systems globally.
  • Encourage mutual recognition of inspections between member authorities.
  • Provide inspector training and international collaboration opportunities.
  • Facilitate information exchange and transparency among member agencies.
  • Support continuous improvement of pharmaceutical quality systems worldwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main purpose of PIC/S?

PIC/S aims to harmonize inspection procedures globally and improve cooperation among regulatory authorities to ensure pharmaceutical quality and safety.

Who are the members of PIC/S?

More than 50 regulatory authorities from countries such as the US, UK, EU, Japan, India, and Australia are members of PIC/S.

What are PIC/S GMP Guidelines?

PIC/S publishes harmonized GMP guidelines used as a reference for manufacturing and quality assurance of medicinal products globally.

Does PIC/S conduct inspections?

PIC/S itself does not conduct inspections but promotes cooperation and equivalence of inspections among its member authorities.
