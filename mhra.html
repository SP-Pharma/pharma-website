About MHRA

The Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) is the UK's regulatory body responsible for ensuring that medicines and medical devices meet applicable standards of safety, quality, and efficacy.

MHRA regulates the UK pharmaceutical industry, including clinical trials, pharmacovigilance, inspections, and marketing authorisations. It provides independent regulatory oversight and supports innovation while protecting public health.

As the UK’s national competent authority, the MHRA plays a crucial role in post-Brexit drug and device approvals and works in collaboration with international regulatory bodies.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is MHRA responsible for?

MHRA regulates medicines, medical devices, and blood components for transfusion in the UK.

How can I check if a medicine is approved in the UK?

You can search MHRA’s Products database for licensed medicines: products.mhra.gov.uk

What is a drug safety update?

It is a monthly bulletin published by MHRA providing safety information on medicines and medical devices.

Where can I find MHRA guidance?

MHRA guidance is available on the UK government's official site under the MHRA guidance collection.

Is MHRA part of the European Medicines Agency?

No, since Brexit, MHRA operates independently of the EMA as the UK's national authority.
