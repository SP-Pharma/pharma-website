About

Health Canada is the federal department responsible for helping Canadians maintain and improve their health. It regulates food, drugs, medical devices, and consumer products to ensure public safety and efficacy.

Health Canada works to minimize health risks from environmental and workplace hazards, supports access to safe and effective health products, and informs the public through safety alerts and recalls.

It also contributes to the development of health policies, guidelines, and standards to promote well-being and innovation in healthcare across Canada.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is Health Canada's role?

Health Canada is responsible for regulating and overseeing food, drugs, health products, and promoting public health.

Where can I check drug approvals?

You can visit the Drugs & Health Products section for product listings, approvals, and advisories.

How does Health Canada issue safety alerts?

Through the Canada Recall & Safety Alerts portal, Health Canada posts official recall information and warnings.

What resources are available to the public?

Health Canada publishes reports, policy documents, scientific research, and educational materials via its Resources page.

Is Health Canada part of the government?

Yes, it is a federal department within the Government of Canada under the Minister of Health.
