About TGA

The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) is Australia's regulatory authority for therapeutic goods including medicines, medical devices, blood products, and biologicals.

TGA ensures these goods meet appropriate standards of safety, quality, and efficacy. It also monitors and enforces compliance, handles post-market surveillance, and provides public safety alerts.

As part of the Department of Health and Aged Care, TGA also supports innovation and international regulatory cooperation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What does the TGA regulate?

The TGA regulates medicines, medical devices, vaccines, and other therapeutic goods used in Australia.

How can I check if a product is approved?

You can search the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG) on the TGA website.

What happens if a product is unsafe?

The TGA can issue recalls, safety alerts, or suspend/cancel products to protect public health.

Where can I find guidance materials?

All guidance documents and forms are under the Resources section of the TGA website.

Does TGA work with global agencies?

Yes, TGA collaborates with agencies like FDA, EMA, and WHO to align on safety and regulatory science.
