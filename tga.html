About TGA

The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) is the regulatory authority of the Australian Government responsible for ensuring that therapeutic goods, including medicines, medical devices, and biological products, are of an acceptable standard of quality, safety, and efficacy.

The TGA regulates the supply, import, export, manufacture, and advertising of therapeutic goods in Australia. It also monitors and investigates safety issues and takes regulatory actions to protect public health.

Main Responsibilities

  • Regulate the supply and advertising of therapeutic goods in Australia.
  • Evaluate new medicines, vaccines, and medical devices for quality, safety, and performance.
  • Monitor adverse events and conduct safety reviews.
  • Enforce compliance with manufacturing and labeling standards.
  • Publish guidance and safety information for consumers and health professionals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does TGA regulate?

The TGA regulates medicines, medical devices, blood and tissue products, and other therapeutic goods available in Australia.

How can I report side effects or product issues?

You can report adverse events and problems with therapeutic goods directly through the TGA’s official website under “Report a problem.”

What is the ARTG?

The Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG) lists all approved therapeutic goods that can be legally supplied in Australia.

Does TGA work with international agencies?

Yes, TGA collaborates with global regulatory authorities such as the USFDA, EMA, and WHO to harmonize safety and quality standards.
