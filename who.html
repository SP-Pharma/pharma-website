About WHO

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. Founded in 1948, WHO works to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable through its leadership on global health matters.

WHO plays a crucial role in setting international health standards, supporting countries in implementing evidence-based policies, and ensuring equitable access to safe and effective medicines, vaccines, and health technologies.

Main Responsibilities

  • Develop international health regulations and guidelines to improve global health outcomes.
  • Ensure access to essential medicines, vaccines, and health products.
  • Strengthen health systems and promote universal health coverage.
  • Coordinate international responses to health emergencies and outbreaks.
  • Provide leadership and partnerships for health promotion and disease prevention.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is WHO’s role in pharmaceuticals?

WHO provides leadership and guidance to ensure that medicines meet global standards of quality, safety, and efficacy through its prequalification and quality assurance programs.

What are WHO prequalified medicines?

WHO prequalified medicines are products that meet WHO’s strict quality, safety, and efficacy requirements for use in international procurement programs.

How does WHO support regulatory authorities?

WHO assists national regulatory agencies in building capacity, improving oversight, and harmonizing standards for medicine registration and monitoring.

Where can I find WHO publications?

WHO publishes reports, research, and guidelines on various global health topics. These are accessible on the WHO Publications Portal.
