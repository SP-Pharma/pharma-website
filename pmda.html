About PMDA

The Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency (PMDA) is a Japanese government organization that works closely with the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW) to ensure the safety, efficacy, and quality of drugs and medical devices in Japan.

PMDA performs reviews of new drugs and medical devices, monitors post-market safety, provides consultations to companies, and develops regulatory science to support innovation and public health.

PMDA is also active in international regulatory collaboration and harmonization efforts.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is PMDA?

PMDA is Japan’s national agency for regulating pharmaceuticals and medical devices, similar to the FDA or EMA.

What does PMDA do?

PMDA reviews applications for drugs and medical devices, ensures safety through post-market surveillance, and offers consultation services.

How can companies get regulatory support in Japan?

Companies can apply for scientific consultations or submit approval applications directly via the PMDA portal.

Does PMDA publish formats or documentation?

Yes, formats and guidance documents can be downloaded from their symposium and forms section.

Where can I find detailed drug information?

You can visit the “Drugs” page for navigation tools and searchable databases related to approvals and reviews.
