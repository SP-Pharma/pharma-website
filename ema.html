About EMA

The European Medicines Agency (EMA) is a decentralized agency of the European Union responsible for the scientific evaluation, supervision, and safety monitoring of medicines in the EU.

EMA supports scientific excellence in the evaluation and supervision of medicines, fosters innovation, and ensures high standards of safety and efficacy for human and veterinary use.

It plays a key role in facilitating the development, authorization, and monitoring of medicines across the EU's internal market, helping to protect public and animal health.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What does the EMA do?

EMA evaluates, monitors, and supervises medicines developed by pharmaceutical companies for use in the EU.

How can I find EMA-approved medicines?

The EMA maintains a comprehensive medicines database accessible through its “Medicines” section.

What is human vs. veterinary regulatory?

Human regulatory covers medicines for people, while veterinary regulatory focuses on animal drugs and treatments.

Where can I follow EMA news?

Visit the EMA “News & Events” section for updates, press releases, and event info.

Is EMA part of the European Commission?

EMA is an agency of the EU but operates independently under the EU’s legal and institutional framework.
