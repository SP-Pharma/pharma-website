ICH History

The International Council for Harmonisation of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) brings together regulatory authorities and pharmaceutical industry to discuss scientific and technical aspects of drug registration.

ICH's mission is to achieve greater harmonisation worldwide to ensure that safe, effective, and high-quality medicines are developed and registered in the most resource-efficient manner.

It focuses on guidelines for quality, safety, efficacy, and multidisciplinary topics that apply across regions and help streamline global regulatory expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is ICH?

ICH is a global initiative that develops harmonized guidelines for pharmaceutical product registration to improve efficiency and safety across borders.

Who are the members of ICH?

Members include regulatory bodies and industry associations from Europe, the US, Japan, Canada, and other global regions.

What are the main guideline categories?

ICH provides guidelines in four areas: Quality, Safety, Efficacy, and Multidisciplinary topics.

How does ICH impact global pharma regulation?

ICH harmonizes regulatory processes, helping companies submit the same data package to multiple agencies, reducing duplication.

Where can I find ICH guidelines?

All current guidelines are available for free on the official ICH website: www.ich.org.
