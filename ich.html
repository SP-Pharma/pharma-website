About ICH

The International Council for Harmonisation of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) is an initiative that brings together regulatory authorities and the pharmaceutical industry to discuss scientific and technical aspects of drug registration. It aims to harmonize guidelines and standards to ensure safe, effective, and high-quality medicines are developed and registered efficiently worldwide.

Main Responsibilities

  • Develop and maintain harmonised guidelines for quality, safety, efficacy, and multidisciplinary topics.
  • Promote international cooperation among regulatory agencies and the pharmaceutical industry.
  • Reduce duplication of clinical trials and data submissions by harmonising regulatory standards.
  • Support the implementation of harmonised guidelines in regulatory systems worldwide.
  • Facilitate continuous improvement of pharmaceutical regulations to meet scientific advancements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of ICH?

ICH aims to harmonise the technical requirements for the registration of pharmaceuticals among major global markets, reducing duplication and ensuring patient safety.

Which regions are part of ICH?

ICH includes regulatory authorities and industry associations from Europe (EMA), Japan (MHLW/PMDA), the United States (FDA), and other regions worldwide.

What are ICH Guidelines?

ICH Guidelines provide harmonised standards covering Quality (Q), Safety (S), Efficacy (E), and Multidisciplinary (M) topics for pharmaceutical product development and registration.

How does ICH benefit the pharmaceutical industry?

ICH helps streamline global regulatory processes, reducing costs, time, and duplication in pharmaceutical development and approval.
