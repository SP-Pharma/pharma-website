About ANVISA

The Brazilian Health Regulatory Agency (ANVISA) is responsible for protecting and promoting public health by overseeing the safety, efficacy, and quality of health-related products and services in Brazil. ANVISA operates under the Ministry of Health and regulates pharmaceuticals, medical devices, food, cosmetics, and other products.

ANVISA also plays an active role in international harmonization efforts, including participation in ICH and PIC/S initiatives, to ensure Brazil’s regulatory framework aligns with global standards.

Main Responsibilities

  • Evaluate and authorize drugs, vaccines, and medical devices for use in Brazil.
  • Ensure compliance with Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP).
  • Monitor and regulate clinical trials conducted in Brazil.
  • Inspect and certify pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities.
  • Provide risk communication and manage post-market surveillance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does ANVISA regulate?

ANVISA regulates pharmaceuticals, biological products, medical devices, cosmetics, food, and other products that affect public health.

How can companies obtain GMP certification in Brazil?

Manufacturers must submit a GMP inspection request through ANVISA’s portal and undergo a formal inspection to receive certification.

Does ANVISA participate in international regulatory programs?

Yes, ANVISA is a member of global initiatives like ICH, PIC/S, and IMDRF to align Brazil’s regulatory practices with international standards.

How can consumers report product issues?

Reports can be filed through ANVISA’s official website using the “Fale Conosco” (Contact Us) section.
