About CDSCO

The Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) is the national regulatory authority of India under the Directorate General of Health Services, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare. CDSCO is responsible for regulating pharmaceuticals and medical devices to ensure their safety, efficacy, and quality for Indian citizens.

Headed by the Drugs Controller General of India (DCGI), CDSCO plays a critical role in approving new drugs, conducting clinical trials, setting standards for drugs, and overseeing the quality of imported and manufactured products.

Main Responsibilities

  • Regulate the import, manufacture, and sale of drugs and cosmetics in India.
  • Approve new drugs, biologicals, and medical devices for market authorization.
  • Monitor adverse drug reactions through the Pharmacovigilance Programme of India (PvPI).
  • Ensure compliance with Drugs and Cosmetics Act and Rules.
  • Promote transparency and digital transformation through online regulatory systems (SUGAM).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the role of CDSCO?

CDSCO is responsible for ensuring that drugs and medical devices in India are safe, effective, and meet the required quality standards.

What is the SUGAM portal?

The SUGAM online portal facilitates electronic submission and tracking of applications for licenses, permissions, and approvals under CDSCO.

Who heads the CDSCO?

The CDSCO is headed by the Drugs Controller General of India (DCGI), who oversees regulatory functions and enforces drug safety laws.

How are clinical trials regulated in India?

Clinical trials are conducted under the supervision of CDSCO in accordance with Schedule Y of the Drugs and Cosmetics Rules and Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines.
