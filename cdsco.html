About CDSCO

The Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) is the national regulatory authority of India under the Directorate General of Health Services, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.

It is responsible for approval of drugs, conduct of clinical trials, laying down the standards for drugs, control over the quality of imported drugs, and coordination of the activities of State Drug Control Organizations.

CDSCO plays a crucial role in ensuring the availability of safe and effective medicines to Indian citizens and promoting transparency in regulatory practices.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is CDSCO?

CDSCO is India's national regulatory body for pharmaceuticals and medical devices, functioning under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.

Where can I check drug information in India?

You can visit the CDSCO portal's “Drugs” section to browse approved drug details.

Is CDSCO responsible for clinical trials?

Yes, CDSCO approves and monitors clinical trials conducted in India as part of its regulatory oversight.

What is the role of CDSCO in medical device regulation?

CDSCO regulates medical devices and diagnostics, ensuring their safety, performance, and quality before they reach the market.

How do I access official notifications or rules?

CDSCO’s Acts, Rules, and Gazette Notifications are publicly available on their website under relevant sections.
